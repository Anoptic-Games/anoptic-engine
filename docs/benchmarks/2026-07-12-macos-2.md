## Notes

Every point rendered exactly at its target per the render column; 3840x2160 dropped as unrealizable on this display, display max 2880x1524 tops the sweep. At 30 s each point clears the five-[profile]-window bar even at the low-fps top of the sweep, so the GPU columns populate at all six rows and the bound reads honestly 〜 this is the run macos-1's Notes anticipated when the 10 s top three came up empty and got mislabeled CPU/present.

GPU-bound at every resolution (w/cap 0.99 to 1.02): the M1's 8-core GPU is the ceiling here, the inverse of the RTX 4090 reference that is CPU/present-bound at every point. avgFPS falls from 145.5 to 38.7 as render pixels climb 0.23 to 4.39 Mpx. GPUms rises 6.764 to 25.646 ms across that same 19x pixel span, sublinear because the shadow-atlas pass is a fixed 8-frustum cost independent of render resolution: at 640x360 that fixed floor dominates (29 ms/Mpx), at 2880x1524 the resolution-scaled passes dominate (5.8 ms/Mpx).

The 1280x720 row carries a maxms of 1005.993 ms, a single ~1 s frame spike. Lone hitch, not a sustained regression: that row's 0.1% low is a healthy 76.4 fps and its avgFPS 90.1 sits on the sweep's smooth curve. maxms is the worst single frame over the whole run and catches exactly this kind of one-off.

Resolution check: swapMiB per megapixel of render pixels holds near-constant at 181, 175, 175, 172, 170, 170 MiB/Mpx across the sweep, the small inflation at 640x360 being fixed allocator overhead on a tiny extent. Top-to-1080p swap ratio 746.8/355.6 = 2.10 tracks the same rows' pixel ratio 4.389/2.074 = 2.12 within ~1%. The six swapMiB values are identical to macos-1, since swap is a deterministic function of the render extent, cross-confirming both runs rendered the same extents. No accounting bug.

ENV_VARS now carries MTL_HUD_ENABLED=1 MTL_HUD_VISIBLE=1, the standard mac Metal HUD that the harness echoes; macos-1 predated the harness adding it. Log: scratch/sweep-macos-2.log.
